Shrimp Poke Bow

By Iskander

Updated 1 December 2024

  • Total Time: 40 minutes
Do you like this recipe?

Number of Ratings:

Poke bowl is a tasty and healthy dish that came to us from Hawaii. It’s the perfect combination of fresh ingredients, seafood and side dishes that allow you to experiment with different flavors and textures. It’s a perfect choice for lunch or dinner, and it’s definitely worth making.

INGREDIENTS

    Shrimp

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/3 cup mayo
  • 2 tablespoons sweet chili sauce
  • 2 teaspoons sriracha
  • 1/2 lime, juiced
  • Pinch of salt

    • Sushi Rice

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 3 tablespoons mirin
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 3/4 teaspoons salt

    • Assembly

  • 1 cup shelled edamame, tossed with a splash of toasted sesame oil and pinch of salt
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced and tossed with a splash of mirin
  • 1 large carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 bunch green onions, chopped
  • 1 avocado, cubed
  • Crispy fried onions
  • Crushed peanuts
  • Extra sauce
  • Sesame seeds

PREPARATION

  • Step 1

    • Cook the rice according to the instructions on the package. Wash the sushi rice thoroughly until the water runs clear. Boil the rice according to the instructions on the package or use our detailed technology for cooking rice for sushi.

  • Step 2

    • After cooking, add rice dressing (rice vinegar, sugar, water), mix well and let cool.

  • Step 3

    • Fry peeled shrimp in a pan, add 1 tbsp. l. soy sauce and unagi sauce. Simmer for 2 minutes.

  • Step 4

    • Peel the avocado and cut it into cubes, cherry tomatoes – in half. Boil the egg, peel and cut in half.

  • Step 5

    • Form a bowl. Place seasoned rice on the bottom of a deep bowl and pour teriyaki sauce over it. Place the shrimp, avocado, cherry tomatoes and egg on top one by one.

  • Step 6

    • Cut the nori into strips, sprinkle with sesame seeds and add sauce if desired.
