Extra-Creamy Scrambled Eggs

By Iskander

Updated 1 December 2024

  • Total Time: 5 minutes
Do you like this recipe?

Number of Ratings:

Adding a small amount of a starchy slurry to scrambled eggs — a technique learned from Mandy Lee of the food blog Lady & Pups — prevents them from setting up too firmly, resulting in eggs that stay tender and moist, whether you like them soft-, medium- or hard-scrambled.

INGREDIENTS

    Yield: 2 servings

  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh herbs (optional, such as chives or parsley)

PREPARATION

  • Step 1

    • Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk them together with the heavy cream until smooth and well combined.

  • Step 2

    • Season the egg mixture with a pinch of salt and pepper.

  • Step 3

    • Heat a non-stick skillet over low heat and add the butter. Let it melt slowly, coating the skillet evenly.

  • Step 4

    • Pour the egg mixture into the skillet. Allow it to sit undisturbed for a few seconds to begin setting.

  • Step 5

    • Using a spatula, gently stir the eggs in a figure-eight motion, scraping the bottom and sides of the skillet.

  • Step 6

    • Continue cooking and stirring over low heat until the eggs are just set but still slightly runny and creamy. Be careful not to overcook.

  • Step 7

    • Remove the skillet from the heat as the eggs will continue to cook from residual heat.

  • Step 8

    • Serve immediately, garnished with fresh herbs if desired. Pair with toast or your favorite breakfast sides.
